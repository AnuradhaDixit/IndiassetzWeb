    • Active Investment: Imagine you're an active real estate investor looking to flip houses. You find a property in need of renovation for $200,000, spend $50,000 on renovations, and sell it for $300,000, earning a profit of $50,000.
    • Passive Investment: Now, let's say you opt for a passive investment by investing $10,000 in a real estate crowdfunding platform which yields a 7% annual return, growing your investment to $10,700 in a year.
    • Comparison: Active investment yields a $50,000 profit from one property, while passive investment grows $10,000 to $10,700 passively in a year.
    • Conclusion: Active investing offers higher profit potential but requires time and risk. Passive investing provides lower returns but minimal involvement.

  2. Can I combine active and passive real estate investment strategies?

    3. Yes, many investors choose to blend active and passive strategies to optimize their portfolios. For example, an investor may actively manage a rental property while also investing in passive vehicles like REITs or real estate crowdfunding platforms to diversify their holdings and reduce risk.
